# Fitness Membership Analytics Challenge

## Overview
You are a data analyst at **MyGym**, a fast-growing fitness center chain in California.  
MyGym has multiple locations and offers **different membership tiers**, **subscriptions**, and **amenities** like group classes, personal training, and multi-location access.  

The goal is to **use data to improve retention, revenue, and engagement**.

## Challenge Goals
Using the dataset, your task is to help MyGym:  
- Identify which **member groups bring the most value**  
- Find opportunities for **pricing, subscription, or service improvements**  
- Improve **customer experience** using data insights  
- Optimize **staffing and facility usage** across locations  
- Analyze trends in **retention, usage, and membership upgrades**

## Business Question
MyGym has a **49% monthly churn rate**.  
We want to answer:
- Which member segments are leaving the most?  
- Which perks or discounts are **not helping retention**?  
- How can we reduce churn to **under 40%** in 90 days?

## Problem Statement
High churn (**49%**) threatens MyGym’s revenue and membership base.  
Even high-value members are leaving, showing the current retention strategy is **not working**.  

**Key Issues:**
- **Elite members** have the highest churn (**51%**)  
- Popular perks like **group lessons** and **sauna** don’t reduce churn  
- The **18-29 age group** contributes **36% of revenue**, making retention critical  

## Key Insights

| Area | Insights |
|------|---------|
| **Overall Churn** | 49% of members leave every 30 days |
| **Membership Tier** | 'Elite' 51%, 'Premium' 50%, 'Standard' 49%, 'Basic' 48% |
| **Perks** | Group lessons 50%, Sauna 48%, Drink subscription 48.8%, Personal training 48% – perks are not improving retention |
| **Revenue by Age** | 18-29 group contributes 36% of revenue ($13.6K), churn 50% |
| **Member Consistency** | 62% visit 3 times/week – strong engagement |
| **Discounts** | 'Loyalty' $13K, 'Promo' $12K – Loyalty members leave faster than Promo users |
| **Geography** | San Jose & SF churn 52%, San Diego 45% |

## Recommendations

### 1. Focus on Elite & 18-29 Members
- Investigate why these members are leaving  
- Conduct surveys or exit interviews  
- Consider an **Elite Retention Specialist** role  

### 2. Re-evaluate Member Perks
- Sauna and group lessons are not keeping members  
- Focus on **3-times-per-week visit habit**  
- Introduce a **"Consistency Challenge"** to reward regular visits  

### 3. Optimize Geography & Discounts
- Learn from San Diego (low churn) and replicate success in high-churn locations  
- Adjust **Loyalty discount** to retain high-value members  

---

This analysis helps MyGym reduce churn, retain high-value members, and improve revenue stability.
